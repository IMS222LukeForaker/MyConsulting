About Me

Luke Foraker graduated from Franklin Community High School as one of the top twenty students in his class and is currently undergoing secondary study at Miami University- specifically in Emerging Technology in Business and Design. His current focus within the field is still unknown, but he hopes to one day incorporate his love for the arts and sciences to study a design career once he finishes his studies. On top of his career focus, Luke also has ties to music and his naturally tuned ear. He is a veteran member of the Franklin Community Blue Regiment Marching Band, serving as a member of the Front Ensemble for six consecutive years, and is now applying his skills to the Miami University Marching Band. Although his love for music may not translate directly to his professional focus, being in music has taught him skills such as quicker reading and comprehension, hand-eye coordination, physical acitivity, spatial awareness, priority management, personal handywork skills, team leadership, stress management, communication, inter-departmental coordination, and in-depth listening skills. This love for music stems beyond the marching arts; Luke has been involved in playing music since the young age of six and has taken positions, roles, and auditions with some of the most prestigious groups in his area. Highlights include auditioning for Phantom Regiment (2021), and recieving a callback invite from the mighty Cavaliers (2023); positions include Percussion First Chair with the Indiana All-State Honor Band for three consecutive years, and first chair in the Franklin Community High School assorted Beginner Band, Symphonic Band, Wind Ensemble, and Percussion Ensemble throughout his four years in high school. His job experience includes a position as a camp counselor at the Young Men's Christian Association's own Camp Tecumseh located in Brookston, Indiana. Here, he exercised skills in team building, leadership, communication, stress management, and event planning in order to create memorable experiences that are fundamental to the camp. He may return to this camp this coming summer to enrich his experience with leadership and connection to his community.
